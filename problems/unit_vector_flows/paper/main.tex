\begin{abstract}
We give a computer-assisted study of nowhere-zero unit-vector flows
($\Sphere$-flows) on cubic graphs, in the form pursued by Jain (first
conjecture) and recently disambiguated by Ulyanov. Three results.
\textbf{(A)} Every nontrivial snark on at most 28 vertices admits an
$\Sphere$-flow, with an independently replayable interval-Krawczyk
certificate. \textbf{(B)} The class of cubic graphs that admit a
constructive $\Sphere$-flow is closed under triangle blow-up and
$3$-edge-cut dot product; starting from the 3\,247 graphs of (A),
the closure $\closure$ contains a strict superset with explicit
operation-tree certificates. \textbf{(C)} Every catalogue snark of
(A) is irreducible under the two inverse operations, so the
catalogue is exactly the base of $\closure$ under these gadgets, and
$\closure$-membership for any cubic graph admits a replayable
decomposition certificate. We also document three independent
obstructions encountered on flower snarks $J_{2k+1}$ and discuss why
they do not extend to a uniform infinite-family theorem by the
methods of this paper.
\end{abstract}
